About
I am Aheer Sravon, a Machine Learning Engineer with a B.Sc. in Industrial and Production Engineering from Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET), where I graduated in 2025. My passion lies at the intersection of graph neural networks, distributionally robust optimization, and reinforcement learning, driven by real-world applications in fraud detection, supply chain management, and competitive market dynamics.
In my undergraduate thesis, I developed a GNN-based framework for fraud detection on transaction graphs, achieving 0.81 AUC on the IEEE-CIS dataset and publishing the work at IEOM 2024. I also published a paper at IEOM 2025 on a variance-based DRO framework that outperforms benchmarks on CIFAR-10-C and Waterbirds by dynamically allocating robustness budgets without group labels. Additionally, I've simulated pricing competitions using RL agents like Q-learning, PPO, and DQN in Gymnasium, replicating theoretical equilibria under demand shocks.
During my internship at AKIJ Textile Mills, I optimized manufacturing processes, suggesting 2.89 million BDT in annual cost savings through statistical analysis and automation. My skills include PyTorch,PyTorch Geometric, Scikit-learn, and operations research tools, complemented by certifications in machine learning and data analytics from Coursera.
I am proficient in English, native in Bengali, and conversational in Japanese. I am pursuing PhD opportunities in robust ML and optimization starting Fall 2026. Explore my projects on GitHub and Kaggle, or connect via LinkedIn.
