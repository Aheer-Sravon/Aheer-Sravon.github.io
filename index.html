About
I am Aheer Sravon, a Machine Learning Engineer with a B.Sc. in Industrial and Production Engineering from Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET), where I graduated in 2025. My passion lies at the intersection of graph neural networks, distributionally robust optimization, reinforcement learning, time series forecasting, and operations research, driven by real-world applications in fraud detection, supply chain management, retail forecasting, and competitive market dynamics.
In my undergraduate thesis, I developed a GNN-based framework for fraud detection on transaction graphs, achieving 0.81 AUC on the IEEE-CIS dataset and publishing the work at IEOM 2024. I authored a preprint which is going to be published in upcoming IEOM 2025 conference on a variance-based DRO framework that outperforms benchmarks on CIFAR-10-C and Waterbirds by dynamically allocating sample-level robustness budgets without group labels via an efficient water-filling algorithm. Additionally, I've simulated pricing competitions using RL and optimization agents like Q-learning, PSO, PPO, and DQN in Gymnasium, replicating theoretical equilibria under AR(1) demand shocks. I conducted a comparative analysis of SARIMA, SARIMAX, LSTM, CNN-LSTM, and Prophet models for predicting daily retail sales, incorporating seasonality and exogenous variables. I also performed a systematic review of 76 studies (2020-2025) on credit card fraud detection, introducing the Deployment Readiness Framework to bridge the deployment-performance gap.
My practical contributions include developing a multi-echelon supply chain simulation for perishable goods using SimPy, optimizing inventory and transportation; during my internship at AKIJ Textile Mills, I optimized manufacturing processes, delivering 2.89 million BDT in annual cost savings through statistical analysis and automation; optimized coal transportation in India using linear programming in R, minimizing costs at 57,013.72 million rupees; and analyzed customer churn in R, identifying key predictors and recommending retention strategies.
My skills include PyTorch, PyTorch Geometric, Gymnasium, statsmodels, Prophet, SimPy, R, Scikit-learn, and operations research tools, complemented by certifications in machine learning and data analytics from Coursera.
I am proficient in English (IELTS Academic Band 7.0), native in Bengali, and elementary conversational in Japanese. I am pursuing PhD opportunities in robust ML and optimization starting Fall 2026. Explore my projects on GitHub and Kaggle, or connect via LinkedIn.
